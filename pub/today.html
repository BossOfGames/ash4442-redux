Today In Japan

Progress, But Not True Equality

Tokyo will be hosting the 2020 Olympic Games. While Japan made substantial progress in the last few decades, there are still groups that are fighting isolation. These groups are now those who are disabled.

Ever since the Pacific War, Japan had in place the Eugenic Protection Law. This law tried to prevent people deemed by the government to be inferior from reproducing. This included physical or mental illnesses. Only recently are people who were affected by the sterilization surgeries taking legal action.1

One thing is for certain. As the definition of "Who is Japanese" is redefined, it only becomes more inclusive every time it does.
