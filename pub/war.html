A Time With Dramatic but Brief Changes

It's impossible to talk about Japanese history without remarking on The Pacific War between primarily the United States and Japan. While there were relatively few changes between Meiji's Japan and Hirohito's Japan for Japanese, there were stark changes on the global stage.

A Clash of National Interests

John Dower remarks that most think The Pacific War was a clash of cultures. However in reality it was a clash of national interests. (Dower 33) To realize the reason why Japan proceeded to go to war, you have to explore external pressures.

Global Depression

The world economy sat in the trough of The Great Depression. As a result, inflation and trade were issues globally.

US Trade Stopped

US Trade Disputes

In a speech he made on October 5th, 1937, Theodore Roosevelt became increasing worried about both Japan and Germany's intentions. To play a passive aggressive approach to influence Japan, the US stopped oil and rubber trade between the nations. Now depleted on critical resources to enable their goal to conquer the Pacific, they decided to attack the US in retaliation. (Huffman 135) (Pearl Harbor Documents)
