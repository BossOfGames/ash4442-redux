Japanese Societal Structure

The Tokugawa period marks the first time we see a modern societal structure within a newly unified Japan. Based on Chinese Confucianism, society under the regime was broken into four classes and arranged hierarchically. (Gordon, p.18)

Outside Of The Structure

However, if one did not fit within the four class structures, they were considered outcasts. These outcasts were delegated work that was considered lower from society, like burials, executions, and the handling of animal carcasses. (Gordon, p.18)
